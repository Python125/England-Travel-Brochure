Attraction Recommendations

Big Ben

"Big Ben" is a nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London, England. The nickname is often used to refer to the clock tower itself, which is officially known as the Elizabeth Tower.

Abbey Road Studios

"Abbey Road Studios" is a recording studio located in London, England. It was established in November 1931 and is best known as the location where the Beatles recorded most of their albums. Abbey Road Studios is considered one of the most famous recording studios in the world and has been used by many famous musicians and bands over the years. It is a popular tourist attraction, and the famous zebra crossing outside the studio has become an iconic image associated with the Beatles.
P.S. Don't forget to walk on The Beatles crosswalk.

Stonehenge

"Stonehenge" is a prehistoric monument located in Wiltshire, England. It is composed of a series of large stones that have been arranged in a circular formation. The stones are believed to have been placed there between 2500 and 1500 BC and are thought to have been used for astronomical, religious, and ceremonial purposes. The exact purpose of Stonehenge remains a mystery, but it is considered to be one of the most important and recognizable prehistoric monuments in the world.

Royal Liver Building

The "Royal Liver Building" is a Grade I listed building located in Liverpool, England. It was completed in 1911 and is one of the most famous landmarks in the city. The building was the headquarters of the Royal Liver Assurance group and is noted for its two clock towers, each with a large bronze statue of the mythical liver bird on top. The liver bird is a symbol of Liverpool and is widely associated with the city. The Royal Liver Building is a popular tourist attraction and is considered one of the most iconic buildings in Liverpool.

Buckingham Palace

"Buckingham Palace" is the official London residence and administrative headquarters of the monarch of the United Kingdom. It is located in the City of Westminster and is one of the most iconic buildings in London. Originally known as Buckingham House, the building was constructed in 1703 as a private residence for the Duke of Buckingham and it has been the official residence of the British monarch since 1837. The palace is also known for its Changing of the Guard ceremony, a traditional military ceremony that takes place at the palace's gates, which makes it a popular tourist attraction.

The Beatles Story

"The Beatles Story" is a museum and exhibition located in Liverpool, England that is dedicated to telling the story of the world-famous band, The Beatles. The museum features a variety of exhibits and displays that explore the band's history, from their early days playing in local Liverpool clubs to their rise to international fame and beyond.

Highclere Castle

Fans of "Downton Abbey" will enjoy coming to "Highclere Castle", a stately home located in Hampshire, England. The castle has a long and interesting history and has been home to the Carnarvon family for over 300 years. Today, visitors can explore the castle's beautiful gardens and grounds, take a tour of the interior, and learn about its fascinating history.

The Making of Harry Potter

The Making of Harry Potter is an attraction located at the Warner Bros. Studio Tour London and is located at the original film studios where the eight Harry Potter movies were filmed over a period of 10 years. Visitors to The Making of Harry Potter can explore authentic film sets, including the Great Hall, Diagon Alley, and the Hogwarts Express, as well as see costumes and props used in the movies. The tour also includes interactive experiences, such as riding a broomstick and trying out the green screen technology used to create special effects.

Food & Drink

Full English Breakfast

The Full English Breakfast is a traditional British meal that typically includes the following items: fried eggs, bacon, sausages, black pudding (a type of sausage made from blood and oats), baked beans, grilled tomatoes, toast, and fried mushrooms. The ingredients are cooked and served together on a plate, usually accompanied by a cup of tea or coffee. The Full English Breakfast is a hearty and filling meal that is often enjoyed on weekends or as a special treat. It is considered to be a classic example of British cuisine and is popular with both tourists and locals.

Fish and Chips

Fish and chips is a popular dish in the United Kingdom, consisting of battered and deep-fried fish served with chips (french fries). The fish is typically cod, haddock, or plaice, which is coated in a batter made from flour, water, and seasonings. Fish and chips are typically served with malt vinegar and salt, and may also be accompanied by sides such as mushy peas, tartar sauce, or lemon wedges. Fish and chips is a staple of British cuisine and can be found at restaurants and take-out shops throughout the UK.

Bangers and Mash

Bangers and Mash is a traditional British dish consisting of sausages (referred to as "bangers") served with mashed potatoes. The sausages are usually made from pork and are grilled or fried. The mashed potatoes are typically made from boiled potatoes that are mashed with butter and milk. Bangers and mash is often served with gravy, which is poured over the sausages and mashed potatoes. This dish is a classic comfort food in the UK and is popular in pubs and homes alike.

Toad in the Hole

Toad in the hole is a traditional English dish that consists of sausages baked in a Yorkshire pudding batter. The dish is believed to have originated in the early 18th century, and its name comes from the fact that the sausages poking out of the batter resemble toads sticking their heads out of a hole.

Tea

In England, tea is typically served hot and in a teapot with milk and sugar on the side. Traditionally, black tea is used and it is often referred to as "English Breakfast Tea". The tea is brewed in the pot and then poured into teacups, which are often accompanied by a small plate of biscuits or pastries. Milk is added to the tea first, followed by sugar, if desired. In some cases, lemon may also be served with the tea. Afternoon tea is a more formal tea service that typically includes a variety of sandwiches, scones, pastries, and, of course, tea.

Crumpets

Crumpets are a type of traditional English bread that is usually served for breakfast or tea. They are made from a batter consisting of flour, yeast, and milk, which is allowed to ferment and rise before being cooked on a griddle or in a pan. Crumpets are usually served warm and can be eaten plain, with butter, or with jam, honey, or other toppings. They have a soft, fluffy texture and a slightly sweet and tangy taste that makes them a popular breakfast or snack food in England.

Scones

Scones are a type of baked good that are popular in the United Kingdom and other countries. They are a type of small, round cake that is typically made with flour, sugar, baking powder, butter, and milk or cream. Scones are usually served with jam and clotted cream, which are spread on top of the scones. Scones can be enjoyed as a snack or as part of a traditional English afternoon tea.

Sunday Roast

The Sunday Roast is a traditional English meal that is typically served on Sundays and is considered a staple of British cuisine. It consists of roasted meat, such as beef, pork, or chicken, accompanied by roasted potatoes, vegetables (such as carrots, parsnips, and Brussel sprouts), gravy, and Yorkshire pudding (a type of savory pudding made from a batter of eggs, flour, and milk). The dish is a favorite among many in England, and is widely available in pubs, restaurants, and homes across the country.

What to Know Before Traveling to England

Geography

England is one of four countries that make up the United Kingdom (along with Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland). It is located in the southern part of the island of Great Britain, and is bordered by Scotland to the north and Wales to the west. The country has a varied landscape, including mountains, hills, and lowland plains, as well as a long coastline.

Climate

The climate of England is generally considered to be temperate maritime, which means that it is relatively mild and wet, with cool summers and mild winters. The country's location in the North Atlantic Ocean, as well as the influence of warm ocean currents like the Gulf Stream, help to moderate the climate and keep temperatures fairly consistent throughout the year.

Weather

England experiences a lot of rain, especially in the western and northern parts of the country, which tend to be wetter than the southeast. The wettest months are typically between October and January, while the summer months (June to August) tend to be drier. Snow is relatively rare in most parts of England, except for the higher elevations in the north and west.

Temperatures

Average temperatures in England vary depending on the season and the region, but generally range from around 4-7°C (39-45°F) in winter to around 18-22°C (64-72°F) in summer. However, temperatures can occasionally reach much higher or lower extremes, particularly during heatwaves or cold snaps.

Cities & Towns in England

England has many cities, including London, Manchester, Birmingham, Liverpool, Nottingham, and Oxford.

England also has many towns, including Bath, Cheltenham, Cambridge, Billingham, Longridge, and Thirsk.

Travel in England

There are several ways to travel in England, depending on your budget and preferences. Some ways to travel in England include taking a train, bus, taxi, riding a bicycle, walking, and driving a car. Keep in mind that in England, people drive on the left side of the road instead of the right.

Language Differences

One important thing to keep in mind is the language differences between the United Kingdom and other English-speaking countries. Some differences include, vocabulary, spelling, grammar, pronunciation, and idioms.

Some exmaples of vocabulary differences include, "biscuit" instead of "cookie," "lift" instead of "elevator," "boot" instead of "trunk," and "chips" instead of "fries."

For spelling, "colour" instead of "color," "centre" instead of "center," "programme" instead of "program," and "travelling" instead of "traveling."

Currency

The British pound, also known as the pound sterling or GBP, is the currency of the United Kingdom. Banknotes in circulation include £5, £10, £20, and £50 denominations, while coins include 1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1, and £2 denominations.

English Culture

Cinema

England has a long-standing history in film production and has produced some of the most iconic and influential films in cinematic history. Some examples include the Harry Potter franchise (2001-2011), James Bond franchise (1962-2021), The Third Man (1949), A Hard Day's Night (1964), If.... (1968), and many others.

Literature

England has produced some of the world's most famous writers, including William Shakespeare, Jane Austen, Charles Dickens, and J.K. Rowling. Some famous examples of literature include the Harry Potter series, Shakespeare's plays, James Bond series (1954-1965), and Sherlock Holmes.

Sports

England has a rich sporting heritage and is home to a variety of sports, some of which originated in the country. Some examples include football (soccer), cricket, rugby, and golf.

Music

England has a rich musical heritage and has produced many influential and successful musicians and bands across a wide range of genres. Examples of popular musical artists include The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Adele, David Bowie, and Oasis.

Art

England is home to many world-renowned art galleries that house collections of both historic and contemporary art from around the world. Examples include the National Gallery, Tate Modern, the British Museum, the National Portrait Gallery, and the Royal Academy of Arts.

Pub

The pub, short for public house, is a type of drinking establishment that is commonly found in the United Kingdom, Ireland, Australia, and other parts of the English-speaking world. Pubs typically serve alcoholic drinks such as beer, cider, and ale, as well as non-alcoholic drinks and often offer simple meals and snacks.

Humour

English humour is often characterized as being dry, witty, and self-deprecating. It is an important aspect of social interaction, and can be seen in a wide range of contexts, from television shows and movies to everyday conversations. Some examples of English humour include Monty Python, Blackadder, and the works of writers such as P.G. Wodehouse and Douglas Adams. The ability to use humour in social situations is often seen as a sign of intelligence and social skill in English culture.
